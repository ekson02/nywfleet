Apostolic function of trans-local influence and networking

  • A. {{ question.questionText }}
    swap_vert
  • A.

    The word apostle means 'sent'

    swap_vert
  • G.

    An apostolic organization takes what God has shown them and sends it to other groups

    swap_vert
  • C

    An apostolic organization connects leaders from different groups to share insights and innovations. They help each other be more effective.

    swap_vert
  • F.

    An apostolic organization analyzes its own internal effectiveness, as well as it effectiveness in influencing other groups. It reflects upon how it is doing and how to better apply principles in other contexts.

    swap_vert
  • B.

    An apostolic organization looks to network leaders and their groups to enhance impact and influence. When a diverse group of people get together, a more balanced set of insights will become possible.

    swap_vert
  • H.

    An apostolic organization considers the nuances of experiences expressed by other network leaders and even “outsiders.” It analyzes and reflects upon them to gain insight into effectiveness. Sometimes this means the development of new types of measurements.

    swap_vert
  • E.

    An apostolic organization looks to connect with unreached peoples, both locally and globally, by focusing on what is universal across cultures and therefore reproducible in any context.

    swap_vert
  • D.
